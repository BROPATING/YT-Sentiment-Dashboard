§ 01

Document purpose

This document specifies what CreatorCore must do, for whom, and under what constraints. It deliberately stops short of how — architecture, framework choices, and detailed schemas come next.

The SRS exists to:

  • Give every team member a shared, unambiguous picture of scope before code is written.
  • Surface disagreements early — when they cost a meeting, not a sprint.
  • Anchor downstream artefacts: architecture diagrams, API contracts, test plans, sales collateral.
  • Serve as the contract by which we judge "is the MVP done."
How to read this

Skim the TOC. Read §02 (Vision) and §04 (Functional Requirements) closely. Treat §13 (Open Questions) as your homework: leave a comment on each one before our next sync.

§ 02

Vision & goals

Problem

YouTube creators above ~25K subscribers receive more comments than they can read, let alone respond to. They lose touch with their audience, miss serious issues (toxicity, spam, brand-unsafe threads), and spend disproportionate time on low-value engagement.

Vision

CreatorCore reads the comments so the creator doesn't have to — and tells them, in plain language, what's worth doing about it.

Primary goals (12 months)

  1. Reduce comment-handling time for an active creator by ≥ 60% versus YouTube Studio alone.
  2. Surface 100% of high-toxicity threads within 30 minutes of appearance.
  3. Generate weekly "Vibe Summaries" that creators rate ≥ 4 / 5 for usefulness.
  4. Onboard 50 paying creators within 6 months of public beta.

Non-goals (explicit)

  • This is not a video-SEO tool (vidIQ / TubeBuddy already own that space).
  • This is not a content-scheduling or thumbnail-A/B tool.
  • We do not support TikTok, Instagram, or X in v1. Focus is the moat.
§ 03

Stakeholders & personas

Three people pay for, use, or are affected by this product. Designing for the wrong one is the most common way SaaS dies.

Primary user

The Solo Creator

30K–500K subs. Edits their own videos. Posts 1–2 times a week. Currently tries to read every comment and feels guilty when they can't.

Needs: a 5-minute weekly view of "what my audience thinks of me," fast triage, no fluff, mobile-friendly.
Power user

The Creator's Manager

Manages comments, brand deals, and community for 1–4 mid-tier creators. Lives in spreadsheets. Will be the loudest advocate or churn risk.

Needs: multi-channel view, bulk actions, exportable reports for client meetings.
Influencer

The Brand / Sponsor

Considers paying a creator for sponsored content. Cares whether the creator's audience is real, positive, and on-brand.

Needs (v2+): shareable "audience health" report the creator can send to land deals.
Internal

CreatorCore Admin

Our own team — support, ops, finance.

Needs: ability to inspect a tenant for support, monitor API quota usage, manage billing.
§ 04

Functional requirements

Each requirement has a stable ID. Use these IDs in commits, PRs, tickets, and tests. Priority is read as P0 = MVP-blocker, P1 = needed before public launch, P2 = post-launch.

4.1 Authentication & channel connection

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-001AuthA user shall be able to create an account using email + password, with email verification.P0
FR-002AuthA user shall be able to sign in with Google (Sign-In with Google).P0
FR-003YT OAuthA user shall authorize CreatorCore to access their YouTube channel via OAuth 2.0 with the minimum scopes required: youtube.readonly, youtube.force-ssl (only if reply-posting is enabled).P0
FR-004YT OAuthA user shall connect more than one YouTube channel to a single CreatorCore account (Manager persona).P1
FR-005YT OAuthA user shall be able to disconnect / revoke a channel; CreatorCore shall purge cached comment data within 30 days of revocation.P0

4.2 Comment ingestion

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-010IngestThe system shall fetch new comments from connected channels via the YouTube Data API v3 on a configurable polling interval (default 15 minutes).P0
FR-011IngestOn first connection, the system shall backfill comments from up to the most recent 25 videos, capped to stay within daily quota.P0
FR-012IngestThe system shall deduplicate comments by YouTube comment ID across polls.P0
FR-013IngestThe system shall capture and store: comment ID, parent ID (for replies), author display name, author channel ID, text, timestamp, like count, language hint.P0
FR-014IngestThe system shall detect when a comment has been edited or deleted on YouTube and update local state on next poll.P1
FR-015IngestThe system shall prioritise polling for videos uploaded in the last 72 hours (where most comments arrive).P1

4.3 Sentiment, toxicity & classification

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-020NLPEach comment shall be classified into a sentiment bucket: positive, negative, neutral, inquiry.P0
FR-021NLPEach comment shall additionally receive a toxicity score on a 0.00–1.00 scale, independent of sentiment.P0
FR-022NLPThe system shall detect comment language with at least the following supported: English, Hindi, Hinglish (Roman-script Hindi).P0
FR-023NLPThe system shall flag suspected spam (e.g. repeated emojis, link spam, bot patterns) as a separate label.P1
FR-024NLPThe creator shall be able to override the AI classification of any comment; overrides shall be used to fine-tune future classifications.P1
FR-025NLPSentiment accuracy shall be validated against a labelled evaluation set (≥ 1,000 comments) before public launch.P0

4.4 Reply assistance

Policy note

YouTube's API Services Developer Policies restrict purely automated comment posting. CreatorCore therefore implements a "draft-and-approve" model: the AI proposes replies, the creator confirms with one tap. Fully autonomous posting is explicitly out of scope until legal review clears it.

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-030ReplyThe system shall identify "low-intent" comments (e.g. "Hi", "First", "Great video") suitable for templated short replies.P0
FR-031ReplyThe creator shall define reply templates with variable slots (e.g. {author_name}).P0
FR-032ReplyThe system shall present drafted replies to the creator for explicit approval before posting to YouTube.P0
FR-033ReplyAn "AI Persona" shall be trainable on a creator's last N approved replies, biasing future drafts toward their voice.P1
FR-034ReplyReply-posting shall be rate-limited per channel to stay well below YouTube's spam-detection thresholds.P0
FR-035ReplyThe system shall log every posted reply with a creator-attributable approval record (audit trail).P0

4.5 Dashboard & analytics

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-040DashboardThe dashboard shall display a sentiment-over-time chart (positive / negative / neutral as stacked area).P0
FR-041DashboardThe dashboard shall display per-video sentiment breakdowns, sortable by recency, view count, and toxicity.P0
FR-042DashboardThe dashboard shall surface top 10 most-positive and top 10 most-negative comments per period.P0
FR-043DashboardThe user shall filter all views by date range, video, sentiment label, language, and toxicity threshold.P1
FR-044Action CenterThe user shall bulk-hide, bulk-mark-as-spam, or bulk-flag-for-review comments matching a filter.P1
FR-045DashboardAll views shall be functional on a mobile viewport (≥ 360px wide).P1

4.6 Insight features (the differentiators)

IDModuleRequirementPri
FR-050AlertsThe system shall send a Toxicity Alert (in-app + email) when toxicity in a video's comment thread exceeds a configurable threshold within a rolling 1-hour window.P0
FR-051Vibe SummaryThe system shall generate a 3–5 sentence LLM summary of the creator's last week of comments, surfacing themes, complaints, and praise.P0
FR-052Vibe SummaryThe Vibe Summary shall cite up to 5 representative comments per theme, linking back to the source.P1
FR-053ROIIf a creator provides affiliate-link metadata, the system shall correlate sentiment cohorts with click-through behaviour.P2

§ 05

Non-functional requirements

IDCategoryRequirement
NFR-001PerformanceDashboard primary views shall render in under 2.0 seconds at the p95 percentile, on a mid-tier mobile network (Fast 3G).
NFR-002ThroughputThe NLP pipeline shall classify ≥ 1,000 comments per minute per worker.
NFR-003AvailabilityTarget uptime: 99.5% during MVP, 99.9% by 6 months post-launch.

NFR-006ComplianceThe product shall remain in compliance with the YouTube API Services Developer Policies at all times. A quarterly review is mandatory.
NFR-007ComplianceThe product shall comply with India's DPDP Act 2023 and, for international users, GDPR (right to access, right to erasure, data portability).

NFR-009CostPer-channel daily YouTube API quota usage shall stay under 80% of the allocated limit during normal operation.

§ 06

External interfaces

YouTube Data API v3

Primary integration. Read access for videos, comments, and channel statistics. Write access for posting replies (only with explicit creator approval). The team must apply for a quota extension before public launch — default 10,000 units/day will not scale.

LLM provider (Vibe Summary & Persona)

One of: Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, or Google Gemini. Cost and rate limits will determine choice; abstract the call behind a provider-agnostic interface from day one.

Payment processor

Razorpay for India-domiciled creators; Stripe for international. Webhook-driven subscription state.

Email / notification provider

Transactional email for verification, alerts, weekly summary. Candidates: Resend, AWS SES, SendGrid.

Authentication providers

Google Sign-In (primary). Email + password (fallback). No social-login fragmentation in v1.

§ 07

Data requirements

High-level data shapes only — full schema lives in the architecture document.

  • User — account credentials, preferences, billing tier.
  • Channel — YouTube channel ID, OAuth refresh token (encrypted), connection status, owner user ID.
  • Video — YouTube video ID, channel ID, title snapshot, published date.
  • Comment — YouTube comment ID, video ID, author, text, timestamp, parent ID. Foreign-keyed to a derived Classification record.
  • Classification — sentiment label, toxicity score, language, spam flag, model version, override flag.
  • ReplyDraft — comment ID, draft text, persona version, status (pending / approved / posted / discarded), approver user ID.
  • VibeSummary — channel ID, period, generated text, source comment IDs, model + cost metadata.
  • AuditLog — actor, action, target, timestamp, IP, user agent.

Retention

Raw comment text retained for the lifetime of the account. On account deletion or channel disconnect, all derived data shall be purged within 30 days, except aggregated, fully anonymous statistics used for product improvement.

§ 08

Constraints & assumptions

Hard constraints

  • YouTube quota — fundamental ceiling on ingestion rate.
  • YouTube ToS — defines what we can and cannot automate (see callout in §4.4).
  • LLM unit economics — Vibe Summary cost must stay below 15% of subscription revenue per active channel.
  • Single-region launch — initial deployment in Asia-South (Mumbai region) for proximity to early users.

Assumptions to validate

  • Creators will pay ≥ ₹999 / $14 per month for this category of tool.
  • Creators trust an external app to post replies on their behalf once approval is in the loop.
  • Hinglish sentiment is solvable to ≥ 70% F1 with available models + a few thousand labelled examples.
  • Polling at 15-minute intervals is fast enough for the toxicity-alert use case.
§ 09

Key user stories

As a solo creator, on Monday morning, I want to read a 5-sentence summary of last week's comments so I can decide what to fix in next week's video — without scrolling for an hour.

As a creator, when a video is going viral, I want to be alerted within 30 minutes if the comment section turns hostile, so I can step in before it shapes the narrative.

As a creator, I want a one-tap "post these 40 friendly replies" button so my new viewers feel acknowledged, while I retain final say.

As a manager, I want to see all my four creators on one screen, sorted by which channel needs my attention today.

As a creator pitching a sponsor, I want a clean, exportable report showing my audience is engaged and on-brand, so my sponsorship rates speak for themselves.

§ 10

MVP acceptance criteria

The MVP is "done" when, and only when, every item below is true. We will not ship sooner. We will not slip past these without an explicit decision.

  1. One creator can connect a single channel via OAuth and see their last 25 videos' comments ingested within 30 minutes.
  2. Each ingested comment receives sentiment + toxicity classification visible in the dashboard.
  3. The dashboard renders sentiment-over-time, per-video breakdown, and top positive/negative comments.
  4. A toxicity alert fires within 30 minutes of a configured threshold breach.
  5. A weekly Vibe Summary is generated automatically and emailed to the creator.
  6. Reply drafts are produced for low-intent comments and require explicit one-tap approval before posting.
  7. OAuth tokens are encrypted at rest. A creator can disconnect and have data purged within 30 days.
  8. The internal sentiment evaluation set shows ≥ 80% F1 (English) / ≥ 70% F1 (Hindi+Hinglish).
  9. Five real creators (recruited during Phase 0 discovery) have used the product for two consecutive weeks and rated the Vibe Summary ≥ 4 / 5 for usefulness.

§ 13

Open questions

Each team member: leave a written take on every question below, before the architecture meeting. No silent agreement.

  1. Self-hosted NLP model (HuggingFace, on our GPU) versus API-based (OpenAI moderation, Google Perspective)? Cost vs. control trade-off.
  2. Which LLM provider for Vibe Summary at MVP — and what's our fallback if they raise prices or rate-limit us?
  3. Pricing model: pure paid, freemium, or free during beta with no commitment? Each shapes feature scope.
  4. Geographic launch market — India-only, or English-speaking creators globally? Affects language priority and payment processor.
  5. Do we need to apply for YouTube API audit / partner verification (required above ~100 users on certain scopes)? Timeline impact.
  6. Polling vs. PubSubHubbub for new-comment detection — what's the latency/cost trade-off in practice?
  7. How do we handle a creator who uses CreatorCore for a channel they don't actually own (manager scenario)? Permissions model.
  8. Should Vibe Summaries be exportable / shareable from day one, or is that a v2 feature?
  9. Mobile-first or desktop-first? (Solo creators are mobile-heavy; managers are desktop-heavy.)
  10. What's our policy when our AI gets it wrong and a creator's reputation is harmed by an approved-but-flawed reply?
§ 14

Glossary

  • Vibe Summary — LLM-generated 3–5 sentence digest of recent comment themes.
  • Toxicity score — 0.00–1.00 measure of how hostile a comment is, independent of sentiment polarity.
  • Low-intent comment — short, formulaic comment (e.g. "Hi", "First", emoji-only) suitable for templated reply.
  • Persona — a creator's voice profile, learned from their approved replies, used to bias future drafts.
  • Quota unit — YouTube Data API's billing unit; each API call costs 1+ units depending on operation.
  • Tenant — a single CreatorCore customer account, isolating their data and preferences.
  • Hinglish — Hindi written in Roman script, often code-mixed with English. Common in Indian YouTube comments.
  • F1 score — harmonic mean of precision and recall; the standard quality metric for our sentiment classifier.
